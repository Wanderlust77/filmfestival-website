Who we are

January 1, 2018 by Jennifer

Brooklyn Vibes Event Co proudly invite you all to the first Brooklyn Outdoor Film Festival at Brooklyn Bridge Park. This year we decided the theme is going to be Brooklyn. You can see 12 films set in Brooklyn or related to all free, all we ask is to preregister to claim your tickets so we can estimate how many people would visit the festival.

How to get to the festival

Brooklyn Bridge Park is accessible by several subway lines (A/C, 2/3, 4/5, F, R) and bus routes (B25, B61, B63, B67). Visitors can also arrive via the NYC Ferry at Fulton Ferry Landing.

If you feel like coming earlier than the film screening, feel free to do so. The following areas are open for public at the moment: John Street, Main Street, Empire Fulton Ferry, Fulton Ferry Landing, Squibb Park and Bridge, Pier 1, Pier 2, Pier 3 Greenway Terrace, Pier 4 Beach, Pier 5, Pier 6, and the Greenway are open to the public right now.

A family-friendly festival

Alcohol cannot be brought into Brooklyn Bridge Park. Alcohol is available for purchase at Fornino at Pier 6 and Luke's Lobster at Fulton Ferry Landing but must be consumed on the premises. As of May 23, 2011, Brooklyn Bridge Park is a smoke-free park, consistent with the recent New York City law. The law is intended to make parks healthier and cleaner places to visit. We ask your compliance with this new rule. Stricly no drugs!

The Grand Opening

December 23, 2013 by Jennifer

We are proudly announce that a Brooklyn local Anne Hattaway kindly agreed to open the film festival. She is going to give a speech from 17:30 before the festival kicks in. Don't miss it!
